Defensive bidding system

Welcome to Chen-Pang He (jdh8)'s defensive bidding system! We use the distribution syntax and abbreviations provided by WBF.

http://www.worldbridge.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/04/Guidetocompletion.pdf

This bidding system is based on Good, Better, Best by Jan Eric Larsson and my improvisation.

Over natural (1X)

  • (1Y)
    • Dbl T/O 11+ HCP or any 17+ HCP
    • 1Z 5+#, 10–17 HCP
    • 1NT 15–17 HCP w/ likely stop
    • 2X 5+#, 12–17 HCP
    • 2Z 5+#, 4–10 HCP
    • 2NT !UNT, 55+ in lowest two unbid suits, 10–15 HCP
    • 3X 7+#, 5–9 HCP
    • 3Y !Gambling, SOL 7+m, at most partial stop
    • 3Z 7+#, 5–9 HCP
    • 3NT !Gambling, SOL 7+m w/ likely stop
  • (1 ♣ )
    • 2 ♣ !Landy, 4+ ♠ , 4+ ♥
      • 2 ♦ !(R), equal preference
  • (1 ♦ )
    • 2 ♦ !Michaels, 5+ ♠ , 5+ ♥
  • (1 ♥ )
    • 2 ♥ !Michaels, 5+ ♠ , 5+m, 10+ HCP
  • (1 ♠ )
    • 2 ♠ !Michaels, 5+ ♥ , 5+m, 10+ HCP

Due to insufficient strength, 1NT by passed hand becomes unusual.

  • (1X)
    • 1NT !UNT, 55+ in lowest two unbid suits

  • (1 ♠ )

Notrump overcalls

  • (1X)-Pass

Like a direct overcall, 1NT by passed hand is unusual.

  • (1X)-Pass
    • 1NT !UNT, 55+ in lowest two unbid suits

Over artificial forcing (1 ♣ )

Our defense was derived from Terrorist's Moscito. Note that we also use this defense even when opponents' 1 ♣ is not necessarily strong (e.g. Polish Club).

We pass strong hands since we are always given a chance to bid again. This is arguably the only sane usage of coming out after a pass.

  • (1 ♣ )
    • Dbl !TRF, 4+ ♥ , 12–16 HCP
    • 1 ♦ !TRF, 4+ ♠ , 12–16 HCP
    • 1 ♥ !NF, 4+ ♥ , 4+m, 5–11 HCP
    • 1 ♠ !NF, 4+ ♠ , 4+m, 5–11 HCP
    • 1NT !NF, 4+ ♠ 4+ ♥ or 4+ ♦ 4+ ♣ , 5–11 HCP
    • 2X 5+#, 5–11 HCP
  • (1 ♣ )-Dbl
    • 1 ♦ !NF (R), 0–2 ♥ , 0–10 HCP
    • 1 ♥ 3+ ♥ , 0–10 HCP
    • 1 ♠ F, 4+ ♠
    • 1NT !INV+ TRF, 4+ ♣
    • 2 ♣ !INV+ TRF, 4+ ♦
    • 2 ♦ !INV+ TRF, 4+ ♥
    • 2 ♥ 4+ ♥ , 0–10 HCP
    • 2NT !INV+, 5+ ♥
    • 3 ♥ 5+ ♥ , 0–10 HCP
  • (1 ♣ )-1 ♦
    • 1 ♥ !NF (R), 0–2 ♠ , 0–10 HCP
    • 1 ♠ 3+ ♠ , 0–10 HCP
    • 1NT !INV+ TRF, 4+ ♣
    • 2 ♣ !INV+ TRF, 4+ ♦
    • 2 ♦ !INV+ TRF, 5+ ♥
    • 2 ♥ !INV+ TRF, 3+ ♠
    • 2 ♠ 4+ ♠ , 0–10 HCP
    • 2NT !INV+, 5+ ♠
    • 3 ♠ 5+ ♠ , 0–10 HCP

Over strong notrump

Recommended by Good, Better, Best, we play (Meckwell) DONT against a strong notrump. We consider a natural notrump opening strong if at least half of the hands contain 15+ HCP. For instance, 14–16 is considered strong but 13–15 is not.

Over the first seat (1NT), we use the original DONT except that the other suit in 2 ♣ is always a major. Responses are natural P/C. Responder can also convert the constructive double to penalty with INV+ hands.

  • (1NT)
    • Dbl 6+X, 10–15 HCP
      • 2X P/C
      • 2NT FG, so unbalanced that defending 1NTx can be dangerous
    • 2 ♣ 4+ ♣ , 4+M, 5–11 HCP
    • 2 ♦ 4+ ♦ , 4+M, 5–11 HCP
    • 2 ♥ 4+ ♥ , 4+ ♠ , 5–11 HCP
    • 2 ♠ 6+ ♠ , 5–11 HCP
    • 2NT 5+ ♦ , 5+ ♣ , 5–11 HCP

We use Meckwell DONT in the other seats. Potential penalty is not that useful since at least one of us has a passed hand.

  • (1NT)
    • Dbl 6+m or 4+ ♠ 4+ ♥
    • 2 ♣ 4+ ♣ , 4+M
    • 2 ♦ 4+ ♦ , 4+M
    • 2 ♥ 6+ ♥
    • 2 ♠ 6+ ♠
    • 2NT 5+ ♦ , 5+ ♣

Over weak notrump

We play Landy against a weak notrump. We consider a natural notrump opening weak if more than half of the hands contain 14 or fewer HCP. This simple defense undermines their systems on approach over (1NT)-2 ♣ . Meanwhile, we still threat to stop in our natural 2 ♦ .

  • (1NT)
    • Dbl PEN, 14+ HCP
    • 2 ♣ !Landy, 4+ ♠ , 4+ ♥ , 12–17 HCP
    • 2X 5+#, 12–17 HCP

By passed hand, we play Meckwell DONT as if against a strong notrump.

Over strong (2 ♣ )

We simply play Mathe with follow-ups same as Landy over (2 ♣ ). The strong 2 ♣ is already self-preemptive, so we don't need to worry about that.

  • (2 ♣ )
    • Dbl !Mathe/Landy, (54)+ in majors
      • 2 ♦ !Equal preference
    • 2X 5+#
    • 2NT !UNT, 5+ ♦ , 5+ ♣

Over natural (2X)

  • (2Y)
    • Dbl T/O 12+ HCP or any 18+ HCP
    • 2Z 5+#, 12–17 HCP
    • 2NT 16–18 HCP w/ likely stop
    • 3X 5+#, 12–17 HCP, FREQ 6+#
    • 3Y !Ask for stopper
    • 3Z 6+#, 15–17 HCP
    • 3NT 19–21 HCP or trick-based w/ likely stop
  • (2m)
    • 4m !Leaping Michaels, 5+ ♠ , 5+ ♥
    • 4om !Leaping Michaels, 5+#, 5+M
  • (2M)
    • 4m !Leaping Michaels, 5+#, 5+oM
    • 4M !STR T/O

Transfer Lebensohl

There are subtle differences among adverse suits. We discuss them case by case.

  • (2 ♠ )-Dbl
    • 2NT !TRF Lebensohl
      • 3 ♣ !NF (R)
        • 3 ♦ ♥ COMP, 4+#
        • 3 ♠ !FG, 4+ ♣ , at most likely stop
        • 3NT !FG, 4+ ♣ w/ stop
      • 3 ♦ ♥ 5+#, 18+ HCP
    • 3 ♣ !TRF INV+ 4+ ♦
    • 3 ♦ !TRF INV+ 4+ ♥
    • 3 ♥ INV 5+ ♥
    • 3 ♠ !Ask for stopper
  • (2 ♥ )-Dbl
    • 2 ♠ COMP, 4+ ♠
    • 2NT !TRF Lebensohl
      • 3 ♣ !NF (R)
        • 3 ♦ COMP, 4+ ♦
        • 3 ♥ !FG, 4+ ♣ , at most likely stop
        • 3 ♠ !FG, 4= ♠ w/ stop
        • 3NT !FG, 4+ ♣ w/ stop
      • 3 ♦ ♠ 5+#, 18+ HCP
    • 3 ♣ !TRF INV+, 4+ ♦
    • 3 ♦ !TRF INV+, 4+ ♠
    • 3 ♥ !Ask for stopper
    • 3 ♠ INV 5+ ♠
  • (2 ♦ )-Dbl
    • 2M COMP, 4+#
    • 2NT !TRF Lebensohl
      • 3 ♣ !NF (R)
        • 3 ♦ !FG, 4+ ♣ , at most likely stop
        • 3M !FG, 4=# w/ stop
        • 3NT !FG, 4+ ♣ w/ stop
      • 3M 5+#, 18+ HCP
    • 3 ♣ !STAY
    • 3 ♦ !TRF INV+, 4+ ♥
    • 3 ♥ !TRF INV+, 4+ ♠
    • 3 ♠ !Ask for stopper
  • (2 ♣ )-Dbl
    • 2X COMP, 4+#
    • 2NT !Reverse Lebensohl
      • 3 ♣ !(R)
        • 3X !FG, 4=# w/ stop
        • 3NT !FG, 4+ ♣ w/ stop
      • 3X 5+#, 18+ HCP
    • 3 ♣ !TRF INV+, 4+ ♦
    • 3 ♦ !TRF INV+, 4+ ♥
    • 3 ♥ !TRF INV+, 4+ ♠
    • 3 ♠ !Ask for stopper

Note that this structure also works for artificial (2m) openings without further modification.
